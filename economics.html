My Approach to Economics

The Four Laws for Economic Recovery

My economic policy proposal at the federal level consists of what the economist Lyndon H. LaRouche in 2014 called his "Four Laws." These measures are fully consistent with the specific intent of the original U.S. Federal Constitution, as specified by U.S. Treasury Secretary Alexander Hamilton.

Urgent Action Required: The only location for the immediately necessary action which could prevent genocide throughout the trans-Atlantic sector of the planet, requires the U.S. government's now immediate decision to institute four specific cardinal measures.

The Four Laws

1 Glass-Steagall Act

Immediate re-enactment of the Glass-Steagall Law, instituted by U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt, without modification as to principle of action.

The Glass-Steagall Act must be restored to separate commercial banking from investment banking and speculative trading. This will protect depositors and prevent the kind of systemic risk that led to the 2008 financial crisis.

2 National Banking System

A return to a system of top-down, thoroughly defined national banking.

We must return to the Hamiltonian system of national banking that can direct credit toward productive investments in infrastructure, manufacturing, and technological development, rather than speculation.

3 Federal Credit System

The use of a federal credit system, to generate high productivity trends in improvements in employment, with the accompanying intention to increase the physical economic productivity and standard of living of the persons and households of the United States.

Federal credit must be directed toward productive economic activity that increases the productive powers of labor and improves living standards, not toward financial speculation.

4 Fusion Energy Development

Adopt a fusion-driver crash program.

A Manhattan Project-style commitment to fusion energy development will provide the technological driver for a new economic platform, creating millions of high-skilled jobs while solving the energy crisis and environmental challenges.

The Historical Precedent

These policies are not untested theories but are based on the successful American System of political economy that built our nation. From Alexander Hamilton's national bank to Lincoln's transcontinental railroad to FDR's New Deal, America has prospered when we have used national credit for productive purposes.

Why This Matters for the Bronx

The current economic system has devastated communities like the South Bronx. By returning to productive credit creation rather than speculation, we can:

  • Fund infrastructure projects that create good-paying jobs
  • Support manufacturing and technological development
  • Invest in education and workforce development
  • Build the foundation for long-term prosperity

International Cooperation

These policies must be implemented in cooperation with other nations committed to mutual development. The era of "America First" nationalism and imperial dominance must give way to a new paradigm of international cooperation based on shared economic development.
